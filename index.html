Future Tournament Ideas

Choose from one of our exciting tournament formats designed to level the playing field and create unique MTG experiences!

Format 1: The Role Reversal (40-Card Constructed)

  • Deck Building: New or inexperienced players construct 40-card decks for veteran players to pilot.
  • The Twist: Beginners get to make strategic decisions about deck composition without the pressure of piloting complex strategies. Meanwhile, experienced players must adapt to unconventional builds and make the best of creative card choices.
  • Tournament Structure: Round Robin format where experienced players compete using the beginner-built decks.
  • Scoring:
    • Deck builders (new players) earn 1 point for every match their deck wins.
    • Pilots (experienced players) earn 1 point per match win.
  • Why It's Fun: This format encourages mentorship, teaches deck building fundamentals, and creates hilarious moments when veterans face unexpected card combinations!

Format 2: Flexible Constructed (40 or 60-Card)

  • Deck Building: All players build their own decks from the available card pool. Choose between a streamlined 40-card deck or a traditional 60-card deck.
  • Strategic Depth:
    • 40-card decks offer greater consistency and faster gameplay, perfect for focused strategies.
    • 60-card decks allow for more variety, complex interactions, and resilience in longer matches.
  • Tournament Structure: Swiss or Round Robin depending on player count.
  • Deck Building Rules: Standard card legality applies (maximum 4 copies of any non-basic land card). Players must declare their deck size before the tournament begins.
  • Scoring: 1 point per match win. Best overall record wins.
  • Why It's Fun: Players can tailor their deck size to their preferred playstyle. Do you value consistency or versatility?

Format 3: Commander Chaos (50-Card, No Restrictions)

  • Deck Building: Bring your own Commander and build a 50-card deck (including your Commander) with absolutely no restrictions on card legality, ban list, or budget.
  • Anything Goes: Banned cards? Legal. Power 9? Bring it. Reserved list cards? Welcome. This is pure, unfiltered Commander chaos.
  • Tournament Structure: Multiplayer pods of 3-4 players, with possible finals for top performers.
  • Deck Building Rules:
    • 1 Commander (can be a legendary creature or planeswalker that can be a commander)
    • 49 additional cards (including lands)
    • Color identity rules still apply
    • No duplicate cards except basic lands (standard Commander singleton rule)
  • Scoring: Points awarded based on placement in each pod (1st place: 3 points, 2nd: 2 points, 3rd: 1 point, 4th: 0 points).
  • Why It's Fun: Compact 50-card decks mean faster games and more consistent draws. No ban list means you can finally play those broken combos you've always dreamed of!

Format 4: Double Commander Mayhem (60-Card, 4x Duplicates Allowed)

  • Deck Building: Choose TWO Commanders to lead your deck and build a 60-card deck with up to 4 copies of any card.
  • The Double Commander Twist:
    • Both Commanders start in the command zone
    • You may cast either Commander at any time (following normal casting rules)
    • Commander tax applies individually to each Commander
    • Your deck must adhere to the combined color identity of both Commanders
  • Breaking Singleton: With 4x duplicates allowed, you can build incredibly consistent combo engines or redundant threats. This isn't your traditional Commander format—it's a high-powered, combo-friendly showdown.
  • Tournament Structure: 1v1 matches or multiplayer pods depending on player preference.
  • Deck Building Rules:
    • 2 Commanders (must both be legendary creatures or planeswalkers that can be commanders)
    • 58 additional cards (including lands) in your main deck
    • Up to 4 copies of any card except basic lands
    • Combined color identity rules apply
  • Scoring: 1 point per match win (for 1v1) or pod placement scoring (for multiplayer).
  • Why It's Fun: Double the commanders means double the strategic options! Build synergistic commander pairs, create unstoppable combos with consistent 4-ofs, and experience a completely unique Magic format.

General Tournament Rules

  • All players must register their deck list before the tournament begins
  • Deck checks may be performed randomly throughout the event
  • Players are expected to maintain good sportsmanship and follow Magic's core values
  • Judges will be available for rules questions and disputes
  • Prize support will be distributed based on final standings

How the Event Works

  • Step 1: Strong MTG players will Draft or Timed Free-Build one Standard deck using the bulk.
  • Step 2: New / low-skill MTG players will play those decks in a Round Robin tournament. Decks are randomized each round as evenly as possible.
  • Scoring:
    • Builders earn 1 point for every tournament win their deck achieves.
    • Players earn 1 point per match win.
